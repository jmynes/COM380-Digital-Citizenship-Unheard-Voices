Enacting Citizenship 4/22 Interview - Anonymous

Introduction to Participatory Politics

My interview subject, unidentified by request, is an actively engaged citizen and activist in Chicago. I conducted our interview over a Google Hangouts call, and have selected some highlights from our discusion for listening below. For the ease and natural flow of language, I will refer to him as Wallace.

Wallace became interested in particpatory politics in late high school, volunteering for a labor union. He was largely focused around the academic aspects, focusing heavily on policy and debating classmates with the intention of winning over his peers rhetorically.

I did a Senior year research presentation on immigration, and the types of abuses that can go on with employers taking people's passports, basically turning them into slaves or indentured servants.

Interview subject, 4/22/2018

Collectives & Engagement

A common thread in our discussion was shedding the weight of being a "policy wonk" full-time, becoming radicalized, and opting instead for direct involvement and activism.

Wallace identified some of his introductory political action early into our conversation, detailing his work with Environmental Justice, a People and Planet First budget rally. The primary demand of this rally was to provide budget for good-paying green jobs, and participation motivated Wallace greatly.

Attending that rally made me feel so empowered... made me feel like there are way more powerful things that I could do as part of a collective

Interview subject, 4/22/2018
